FitData

Overview

FitData is a project for a Human-Computer Interaction Design course done in a group of three members. The design problem involved tackling the problem of getting the public involved in health science research. In order for scientists to collect data, they resort to conducting experimental trials and surveys on subjects, who can be difficult to recruit at times.

FitData offers an online platform for scientists to create survey questions for a particular health science research topic and then allow any user to contribute to the survey questions at their own pace.

My Role

I contributed to the user research phase of the project by carrying out the observations and interviews and compiling the results to figure out the design problem and the user needs.

After that, I contributed to the front-end development of FitData by starting out with low-fidelity wireframing and iteratively improving the design through user testing until high-fidelity prototypes were created.

Lastly, I contributed to the user testing of the beta version using Google Analytics to carry A/B testing.
